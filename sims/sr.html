The Intuition Trick
Look at the numerator: \(u + v\). That is exactly what your gut says to write. Pick the signs of \(u\) and \(v\) to match your physical intuition — same direction → both positive, opposite → one negative. Then be consistent with those same signs for \(\beta_u\) and \(\beta_v\) in the denominator. The denominator automatically enforces \(|w| < c\). It never fails.
Boost Interpretation
A boost into S′ = we jump into a frame moving at \(+v\) relative to S. The addition formula converts an object's velocity \(u\) measured in S′ into \(w\) measured in S.

The inverse transformation is a boost into a frame moving at \(-v\) — just replace \(v \to -v\) everywhere. That takes you from S back to S′.

Proper Time \(\Delta\tau\)
Two events at the same node (same spatial location). A single clock at that node records both — no travel, no ambiguity. This gives the minimum time interval between the events that any frame will ever measure: \(\Delta\tau = |t_B - t_A|\).
Proper Length \(L_0\)
Two events at different nodes but at the same local clock time (simultaneous in this frame). The ruler laid between the two nodes gives the proper length — the maximum spatial separation that any frame will measure.
