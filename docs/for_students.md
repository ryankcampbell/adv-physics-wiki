# Advanced Physics Wiki — Student Guide

## What is this?

The Advanced Physics Wiki is a project where **you** build the course reference material. Instead of reading a textbook, you research a physics concept, write an explanation, build an interactive simulation, and publish it for your classmates. Your classmates then test your explanation and build homework problems based on it.

By the end of the course, the class has produced a complete wiki — written entirely by students.

---

## The three stages

Every Insight Card goes through three stages before it's published.

### Stage 1 — IC (Insight Card)

You write a research-based explanation of a physics concept. Your IC has six sections:

1. **Research Question** — the specific question you investigated (e.g. *"Why does time slow down for a moving clock?"*)
2. **Explainer** — your explanation of the concept in your own words, aimed at a classmate
3. **Simulation** — an interactive visualization you build with the AI sim builder
4. **Citations** — sources you used (papers, textbooks, your classmates' published ICs)
5. **Limiting Cases** — two edge cases that test the boundaries of the concept
6. **Simulation Caption** — a short guide for how to interact with your simulation

When you're done, submit via the **Submit to Class Drive** button. Your teacher reviews it in their inbox and either approves it (moves you to AT stage) or sends revision notes.

---

### Stage 2 — AT (Adversarial Test)

Now you switch roles: you become a *reviewer* of someone else's published IC (usually your own concept, with your teacher's IC as the reference).

Your job is to **challenge** the IC — try to break the simulation, find edge cases the author missed, or test claims in the explainer. You fill out:

- **Limiting Cases** — verify the two limiting cases work in the sim
- **Stress Case** — push the simulation to an extreme value
- **Reviewer Info** — your name, verdict (pass / pass-with-notes / fail), and a brief summary

Submit when done. Your teacher reviews and either approves (moves you to HW stage) or sends notes.

---

### Stage 3 — HW (Homework Questions)

Now you write a homework problem based on the concept — something a future student would solve on an exam. You work with the AI to draft:

- A **stem** (the scenario setup)
- One or more **parts** with specific questions
- An **answer key** for each part

The AI knows your research question and explainer, so it can help you write a question that matches the level of your IC. Submit when you're happy with it. Your teacher approves it into the question bank.

Once approved, your IC is **published** — your name appears on the concept navigator and your IC, AT report, and homework question are all visible to the class.

## How to log in

1. Go to the URL your teacher shares (something like `http://192.168.x.x:3000`)
2. Click **Student Login** and enter your name and password exactly as given
3. You'll land on **My Insight Cards** — your personal workspace

---

## Tips

- **Save often** — the editor auto-saves to your browser, but use the ↓ Save Draft button before closing a tab
- **The sim builder is powerful but iterative** — describe what you want, see what it makes, then ask it to adjust. A few rounds is normal
- **Citations matter** — link to the specific IC or paper you drew from, not just a homepage
- **The AI in AT stage is Socratic** — it won't just tell you if you're right. It'll ask questions. Engage with it
- **The AI in HW stage is collaborative** — describe the scenario you have in mind and it will help shape it into a good problem
- **Your teacher sees everything** — drafts aren't private once submitted

---

## What if something breaks?

Use the **Report Bug** button in the top navigation bar. It saves a note to your teacher with the current page and time. Don't lose your work — download a draft backup first with the ↓ Save Draft button.
