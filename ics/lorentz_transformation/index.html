Citations

A. Rodriguez — Galilean Velocity Addition and the Problem with Light
Purpose: We used Rodriguez’s result that Galilean addition breaks down for light to motivate why the transformation law for coordinates must also change — the Galilean boost is the direct coordinate analogue of Galilean velocity addition. If the velocity rule fails, the coordinate rule must fail too.
Jimmy Chen — Spacetime Diagrams: Drawing Relativity on Paper
Purpose: The spacetime diagram gave us the geometric picture of what the Lorentz transformation does: it tilts the coordinate axes, which is exactly the hyperbolic rotation interpretation we use in our explainer. Without the diagram it would have been harder to see why the transformation mixes space and time.

Key Idea

\n
\n The Lorentz boost squeezes both axes toward the 45° light line. Faster speeds (larger |β|) squeeze more.

\n new x̂ → the new spatial basis
\n new t̂ → the new temporal basis

\n β > 0: forward transform Λ (S → S′)
\n β < 0: inverse transform Λ⁻¹ (S′ → S)

\n The grid shows how the primed coordinate system appears on an unprimed spacetime diagram.\n
