The Lorentz Transformation

Research Question
What does the Lorentz transformation actually transform — and why is it fundamentally different from a Galilean boost, even though it reduces to one at low speeds?
Explainer

Start with what we already know. In Newtonian mechanics, when frame S′ moves at constant velocity along the -axis relative to frame S, the Galilean transformation tells us how to convert coordinates: and, crucially, . Time is the same for every observer. Space transforms, but time is left completely untouched — it is a universal background parameter that ticks identically for everyone.

Special relativity dismantles that picture. The two postulates — that the laws of physics are the same in all inertial frames, and that the speed of light is constant for all observers — force us to abandon absolute simultaneity. If the same light pulse must travel at as measured by both S and S′, then the transformation connecting them cannot leave time alone. Space and time must mix.

Citations
  1. [1] A. Rodriguez, Galilean Velocity Addition and the Problem with Light
    We used Rodriguez’s result that Galilean addition breaks down for light to motivate why the transformation law for coordinates must also change — the Galilean boost is the direct coordinate analogue of Galilean velocity addition. If the velocity rule fails, the coordinate rule that underlies it must fail too, and a new transformation is needed.
  2. [2] S. Park & A. Rodriguez, Spacetime Diagrams: Drawing Relativity on Paper
    The spacetime diagram from this IC gave us the geometric picture of what the Lorentz transformation does: it tilts the coordinate axes, which is exactly the “hyperbolic rotation” interpretation we use in our explainer. Without the diagram it would have been harder to see why the transformation mixes space and time rather than rotating them independently.
Connection to Anchor Question
The Lorentz transformation is the mathematical engine of the twin paradox resolution: it is the tool you apply at each leg of the traveling twin’s journey to convert between the rocket frame and Earth frame, and the asymmetry in proper time falls directly out of those calculations.
