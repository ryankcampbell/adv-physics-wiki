The Lorentz Factor γ: One Number to Rule All of Special Relativity
by Alex Rodriguez Published Special Relativity

Research Question

Where does the Lorentz factor γ come from, and why does a single number control both time dilation and length contraction?

Explainer

Special relativity rests on two postulates: the laws of physics are the same in all inertial frames, and the speed of light c is the same for every observer regardless of relative motion. These two simple rules collide head-on with Newtonian intuition — and the collision produces γ.

The light-clock thought experiment. Imagine a clock built from two mirrors with a photon bouncing between them. In its rest frame, the photon travels a distance L each tick, so the tick period is t₀ = L/c.

The factor out front is γ. It emerges directly from insisting light travels at c in every frame.

1Define β = v/c (the velocity as a fraction of light speed, always between 0 and 1 for massive objects).
2γ = 1/√(1 − β²). At v = 0, γ = 1 (no relativistic effects). As v → c, γ → ∞.
3Time dilation: t' = γ t₀. Moving clocks run slow by exactly γ.
4Length contraction: L' = L₀/γ. Moving rulers shorten by exactly γ.

The same γ appears in both effects because both follow from the same geometric constraint on how spacetime intervals must behave to keep c constant. It also appears in relativistic momentum (p = γmv) and energy (E = γmc²), which is why it truly governs all of special relativity.

Why can't you reach c? As v → c, γ → ∞, so the relativistic kinetic energy K = (γ − 1)mc² also diverges. Reaching exactly c would require infinite energy. This is a physical impossibility for any object with mass — not an engineering limitation.

Citations

Feynman Lectures on Physics, Vol. I §15 — Special Relativity
Primary source for the light-clock derivation and the physical meaning of γ. Feynman's treatment is unusually clear about why both postulates together force the existence of γ.
Einstein, "On the Electrodynamics of Moving Bodies" (1905)
Original paper. §4 derives the transformation of clock rates directly from the postulates — this is where γ first appears, though Einstein didn't call it that.
HyperPhysics — Lorentz Transformation
Used to cross-check the numerical values of γ at specific velocities and to verify that the same factor appears in length contraction as in time dilation.
