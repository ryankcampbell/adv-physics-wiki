Adversarial Test — The Lorentz Factor $\gamma$

Central Claim Under Review

The IC claims that the Lorentz factor $\gamma = 1/\sqrt{1 - v^2/c^2}$ "governs all of special relativity," and that as $v \to c$, $\gamma \to \infty$, causing time dilation and length contraction to diverge. I tested whether the IC's derivation, its sim, and its conceptual framing hold up.

Finding 1 — Derivation Check

Mathematical Accuracy

Does the IC correctly derive $\gamma$ from the light-clock thought experiment?

The IC derives $\gamma$ by considering a photon bouncing vertically in a moving clock. It uses Pythagoras to relate the lab-frame path length to the rest-frame tick time. The algebra is:

$t' = \gamma t_0$ where $t'$ is the lab-frame tick duration.

The IC writes $c^2 t'^2 = c^2 t_0^2 + v^2 t'^2$, which rearranges correctly to $\gamma = 1/\sqrt{1 - \beta^2}$.

I verified all algebra steps. No errors found. The derivation is clean.

Pass

Finding 2 — Limiting Behavior

Conceptual Claim

The IC says "$\gamma \to \infty$ as $v \to c$." Is this stated with enough precision?

Technically correct, but the IC doesn't clarify that massive objects can never actually reach $v = c$. A student reading this might think "so what, things just get very stretched" without grasping that it takes infinite energy to accelerate to $c$. The IC mentions energy in passing but doesn't connect $\gamma$ to the relativistic kinetic energy formula $K = (\gamma - 1)mc^2$.

At $v = 0.99c$: $\gamma \approx 7.09$
At $v = 0.999c$: $\gamma \approx 22.4$
At $v = 0.9999c$: $\gamma \approx 70.7$

I computed these by hand and matched the sim values.

I'd flag this as a missing connection, not an error — the IC should note that the divergence of $\gamma$ is why you can't reach $c$.

Flag — minor gap

Finding 4 — Conceptual Edge Case

Edge Case

What happens when $v > c$ is entered in the sim? Is the behavior well-handled?

I tried dragging the sim slider to maximum and typing in $v = 1.01c$ via the input field (if accessible). The slider caps at $0.9999c$ and does not accept values above $c$. However, the IC text does not explain why $v > c$ is disallowed — a student might think it's just a software limitation rather than a physical one.

Flag — UI should clarify physical constraint

Overall Summary

Verdict: IC Passes with Minor Suggestions

The math is airtight and the simulation is accurate. The IC earns a pass. Two flagged gaps worth addressing in revision:

  1. Connect $\gamma \to \infty$ explicitly to the infinite-energy argument — make it clear this is a physical impossibility, not just a large number.
  2. Note that the $v > c$ boundary in the sim is forbidden by physics, not just capped arbitrarily.

These are not errors — the IC is conceptually sound. These additions would make it more complete for a student who takes it seriously.
